Nikola Tesla was born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, a village in the Austrian Empire, in what is now Croatia. He was a brilliant inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist best known for his contributions to the design of the modern alternating current electrical supply system. Tesla worked briefly for Thomas Edison before striking out on his own. He developed the Tesla coil, an electrical resonant transformer circuit, and made pioneering contributions to the development of alternating current machinery, including the polyphase AC motor. Tesla held over 300 patents and his innovations include contributions to radio technology, remote control, radar, and wireless energy transfer. His work on AC power systems, developed in partnership with George Westinghouse, won the "War of Currents" against Edison's direct current system and became the global standard for electrical power transmission. Tesla also conducted bold experiments in wireless communication and envisioned a worldwide wireless communication system. Despite his extraordinary contributions, Tesla spent his final years in financial difficulty and died on January 7, 1943, in New York City.
